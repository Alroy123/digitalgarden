**Kennedy Folk:**

Kennedy never knew his parents; he was an orphan in the city of Carpree, one of the six nations of Larin. He had nothing to his name and was forced to sell a euphoric drug known as Kranks, for the head master of the orphanage named Forgin. As he would walk the streets of Carpree he would look at the wealthy side of town with their fine clothes and nice things and wish that one day he would have all that. So he started skimming from the money had made for Forgin. Over a couple years he gathered a decent amount of extra money, but Forgin finally figured him out and tried to kill him. Kennedy had to use almost all he earned to charter a boat to Dalrrin just to save his life. He was back where he started all those years earlier, poor and alone, no food in his belly. And in his darkest hour that's when it came. The being known as “The Entity” sitting on a large tree that has been carved to be a throne, his body made of twisting vines and greens. His eyes glowing orange like pools of magma. The Entity gave Kennedy power, and in return asked for the power of the things Kennedy killed to be given to him. 

  

After that moment Kennedy’s life changed forever, he had power and he was getting wealth from being a bounty hunter. He made a nice life for himself and he believed that nothing could be better than this, until he met Lillian, and the rest of the Frightful Four. 

  
  

**Lillian Ashguard:**

All of Lillian's life was chosen for her even before she was born. Her parents were among some of the most devout followers of Cyborus, and they had visions of their daughter being a great leader in the clergy. But when Lillian was born she was a spit fire, she was as wild as an untamed forest fire. Her parents tried to help her find her inner peace to calm her emotions and open her third eye of Cyborus. But she never could, the quiet drove her mad and stillness made her fidget. All she wanted to do was make her parents proud and serve Cyborus as her family has done for generations. 

The only place she could find calm and peace was swinging a sword, a fact she had to hide from her parents because they believed violence to be a slippery slope to losing one's peace, and that the laws of fate can be brought in balance through other means and not the blade. But all Lillian wanted was to be a Paladin and go on adventures. And after her parents found out that she was practicing with a blade they grounded her in her room for a month, that's when Lillian decided if her parents wouldn't let her be how she wanted to be she would run away and become an adventurer herself. So she gathered some of her things that were worth money in her room and sold them to buy armor and a sword and shield and set off to whatever awaited her. Not stopping for nothing or no one letting her blade do all the talking until she met a stranger and intriguing older tiefling named Kennedy. 

  
  
  

**Oko:**

Oko was the product of a dragons cult, born of the blood of mortals and the blood of a brass dragon. She spent her whole life in captivity, she was used as a source of blood for any rituals the cult performed, she was a tool not a person. She spent most of her time in a dark cave the cult called her “world”; the only light came from a crack in the wall. The only source to the outside world, all she could see was a small patch of wildflowers in a meadow from a nearby cave. These flowers were the only feeling of happiness in her life. Even though she was gifted with arcane abilities the constant draining of her blood left her fatigued and the only possible magic she could make was a splash of acid, but she could feel something more from within. Over 29 years of her life was within these cave walls serving the whim of this cult and all she wanted to do was reach those wild flowers. And that day would come when a group of people killed the cult and set her free, she would finally be able to go to the wild flowers. When she got to the patch the flowers were destroyed in the recent battle, burned completely away. Oko had nothing, her life as she knew it and wanted it to be was gone. That's when Kennedy and the rest offered to take her in. 

  
  
  

**Twig Leafwind:**

Twig was born to wealth and prestige, his family was one of the more influential families in all of the Hannis Bay. Even with all the wealth at Twig’s fingertips all he wanted to be was a pirate, he wanted the thrill of adventure. One day while he was watching the ships come into port he saw it, the most beautiful ship he had ever seen. A large galleon carved from dark wood with pure white sails and a large golden eagle at the front of the ship. It was the Golden Horizon captained by none other than the great pirate captain; Captain Orin. Twig had to get on that ship, so he snuck into his father's office, cracked his safe and stole his inheritance to charter passage. He found Orin at a seedy pirate bar down the dock and tried to convince Captain Orin and his crew to take him with them, but they laughed at him for he had nothing to show his worth to them other than gold. That's when the news broke that someone stole from the Leafwinds and stole gold out of Twigs father's safe. That's when Twig twisted the truth a bit and left out a few details but took credit for the steal saying that he was the one to steal from the Leafwinds. Amazed at this display of thievery, Captain Orin let Twig join his crew. For months he worked on the ship as a deckhand, until one day a storm knocked Twig overboard and into the raging storm. He probably would have died if it wasn't for a passing vessel that pulled him on board and where he met Kennedy for the first time. 


**The Cult of the Brass Dragon**

The party was making their way towards Sinderville when Kennedy noticed it long before the others did—a thin, dark line rising against the horizon where no village yet something was burning. He stood at the edge of the road, eyes narrowed, something deep within him stirring. The Entity did not speak, but it pulled.

“Something’s there,” Kennedy muttered.

Lillian rested her hand on the hilt of her sword, already alert. “Then we go there. If people need help, we don’t stand around.”

Twig groaned from where he leaned against a tree. “Or it’s a trap. Or worse—nothing worth the trouble.” He glanced between them, then sighed. “…but fine. Adventure, right?”

The cave system hidden beneath the hills had long been forgotten by the world above. To the cult, it was sacred ground. To Oko, it was a prison. Chains rattled softly as she shifted. Another ritual had just ended. Her strength was fading again.

“Prepare her,” one of the cultists said. “The next offering must be stronger.” 

Oko barely heard them. Her eyes were fixed on the crack in the stone wall. The flowers were still there.

The party arrived at the area that was burning, though it was not burning from fire but from acid. Scorched bodies tied to stages driven into the ground . And a ritual summoning circle marked with strange symbols carved deep into the dirt. The cult was trying to summon the spirit of a long dead evil dragon. He knelt, to read one of the carvings.

“Dragon worship,” he said quietly.

Lillian frowned. “Then we end it.”

Twig peeked inside a cave along the mountain by the ritual, immediately stepping back. “That is definitely a cult cave. I’ve seen enough bad decisions to recognize one.”

Before the party could go into the cave a small group of cultists stepped out with a bowl of blood and a screaming person that they were gonna use as a sacrifice.

“Please—help me!” the victim cried, struggling against their grip.

The lead cultist raised the bowl. “The offering must be made—”

Lillian didn’t let him finish, She charged. Steel flashed as her blade sliced the cultist forearms in half, knocking the bowl to the ground and sending dark blood spilling across the dirt. 

“You’re not taking another life today!” she shouted, her voice fierce. 

She then drove the blade deep into the cultist chest, Twig moved the moment chaos broke loose, slipping wide around the fight. 

“Alright, I guess we are starting” he muttered,

He ducked behind one of the robed figures and with a quick strike he drove his daggers into the side of their neck stopping the chanting. The remaining cultists scrambled, one drawing a jagged dagger while another tried to drag the victim away.
Kennedy stepped forward slowly, the air shifted slowly around him. Something unseen coiled around him as he raised his hand. When the cultist lunged, Kennedy caught him—and for just a moment, something drained. The man’s strength faltered, his attack weakening mid-strike.

“What… are you?” the cultist gasped.

Kennedy didn’t answer. “More…” the Entity whispered faintly.

One cultist broke and ran for the cave.

“They’re warning the others!” Twig shouted.

Lillian turned, sprinting forward, and cut the cultist in half before they could make it inside. She stood there for a moment, breathing heavily, the adrenaline rush. And when it faded she just stared at what she had done—then shook it off.

“We’re too late to be quiet,” she said. “We finish this.”

Behind them, the rescued victim collapsed, shaking.

“Th-thank you…” they managed.

Twig gave a quick nod. “Yeah, yeah, try not to get sacrificed again.”

Kennedy’s gaze was already on the cave entrance, “They know we’re here now,” he said.

Lillian stepped beside him, lifting her blade, “Good.”

The deeper they went, the worse it became. The air was thick with iron and incense. More chanting echoed through the tunnels, low and rhythmic. The walls themselves seemed alive with carved scales and draconic imagery. They found the first group of cultists in a chamber with several people in cages waiting to be the next sacrifice. The cultists were stripping all that the captives had off, ripping their clothes, shaving their heads with crude and dirty implements. Lillian didn’t hesitate.

“For Cyborus!” she shouted, her voice shaking but strong.

She jumped on three cultists with all her and her armor's weight, sending them to the group. Then rushed another cultist carving a huge gash from shoulder to hip, then punching him in the crotch with her gauntlet for good measure. 
Twig ducked behind a pillar, then struck from the shadows, quicker than he looked. 

“Okay—this part I like!” he shouted 

He then threw daggers out to the ones Lillian tackled to the flood, plunging them deep into their necks.
Kennedy stepped forward last, something inside him hungered. 
Vines—thin, spectral, lashed out from his shadow as he struck, draining the life from a cultist who never even saw him move.

“More…” a voice whispered in his mind.

Kennedy clenched his jaw. “Not now.”

They pushed deeper, room after room, the cult resisted—but they were not prepared for the strength of this party. At the heart of the cave, they found her. She barely looked like a dragonborn anymore. Thin, chained to a crude altar. Her arms were marked with countless cuts. The air around her faintly shimmered with unstable magic.

Lillian froze. “Gods…”

Twig swallowed. “…that’s what they’ve been doing?

Oko lifted her head weakly, her glowing eyes locking onto them, “Are… you real?”

The chamber trembled as the cult leader stepped forward, his form illuminated by firelight and something far more unnatural. Brass scales adorned his body, his skin marked with draconic runes that pulsed like a heartbeat. Behind him, a massive, half-formed dragon shape of flame and shadow coiled against the cavern walls.

“You dare interrupt the ascension?” The cult leader slammed his staff into the ground.

Flames erupted in a circle around him as the remaining cultists emerged from the shadows, throwing themselves into the fight.

“Protect the ritual!” one screamed.

Lillian charged head-on, her strikes cleaved limb from limb, forcing her way toward the center. She was fueled with emotion. 

“No more of this!” she shouted.

Twig darted between stone pillars, using the chaos to his advantage. 

“Big scary ritual, robed lunatics—yeah, this is definitely above our pay grade,” he muttered, slicing at exposed backs and vanishing again before retaliation could come.
Kennedy hung back for just a moment, watching. Feeling.

“So much to take…” he thought.

He stepped forward—and the shadows answered. Each strike he made seemed to pull something unseen from his enemies, leaving them weaker, slower.

“Don’t lose yourself,” Lillian called to him, noticing the shift.

He didn’t respond.

With a roar, the cult leader raised both arms. The half-formed dragon behind him surged forward, its body snapping into partial reality—massive claws slamming into the cavern floor, acid spilling from its jaws. Acidic heat warped the air. Stone cracked beneath its feet.

“You will witness rebirth!” the cult leader bellowed.

A wave of acid tore across the room.

Twig barely dove out of the way. “Alright! That’s new—that’s very new!”

Lillian stood her ground, lifted her shield. Bracing through the acid, armor glowing from the heat. “We push through!”

The dragon’s presence strengthened the cult leader, his movements faster, his strikes heavier. Each attack felt like it carried the weight of something ancient behind it.

Kennedy felt it again, the pull stronger than ever.

“Take it,” The Entity whispered, no longer subtle. “Take it all.”

Kennedy hesitated.

The dragon roared again, knocking Lillian back. Twig stumbled, barely keeping his footing.

Oko—still chained—looked up weakly, “Please…” she whispered.

Something inside Kennedy snapped, stepping through the acid towards the cult leader. The shadows of branches around him exploded outward—twisting vines and dark tendrils tearing across the battlefield, lashing onto the dragon, the cult leader, anything they could touch. The cult leader screamed as his strength began to drain rapidly.

“No—this power—it is not yours!”

“It is now,” Kennedy said, his voice colder than before.

The dragon faltered, its form destabilizing as its energy was ripped away.

“Lillian NOW!” Kennedy screamed.

She saw the opening, and took it. She surged forward, channeling everything she had into one divine strike. Her blade cut deep into the cult leader’s chest. At the same moment, Twig struck from behind, driving his weapon into a gap in the leader’s defenses. 

“Should’ve stayed in the bar,” he muttered.

Kennedy reached out—and took the rest. The cult leader collapsed, his body withering as whatever power sustained him was stripped away. The dragon behind him let out one final, echoing roar before unraveling into a pile of acidic mush.

Silence.

The shadows around Kennedy slowly receded.

Lillian approached carefully. “…Kennedy?”

He didn’t answer right away. When he finally looked up, something in his eyes lingered—something not entirely his own. Then it was gone.

“It’s over,” he said.

Twig rushed to Oko first, picking the lock on  the chains with trembling hands. “You’re safe now. We’ve got you.”

Oko didn’t move at first. Then, slowly: “The flowers…”

They helped her outside. The sunlight hit her like a dream. She stumbled forward, toward the meadow she had watched her entire life and stopped.

Gone. Nothing but acid riddled fields.

“No… no, they can’t be gone,” Oko whispered, falling to her knees.

No one spoke. Twig looked away, Lillian’s grip tightened on her sword, and Kennedy held out a hand.

“You don’t have to stay here,” he said. “Come with us.”

Oko looked up at him, tears cutting through the dirt on her face. After a long moment, she took his hand. And just like that, four broken paths became one, they became the Frightful Four! 

  
  

**A Battle Hard Fought**

The job sounded simple, a merchant caravan had gone missing along the edges of the Candin Wilderness, a dense stretch of forest known for strange paths and even stranger rumors. The request was clear—find the caravan, recover what remained, and deal with whatever caused it.

“Missing caravan in a creepy forest,” Twig said, adjusting his gear. “This feels like the part where we say no.”

Lillian shook her head. “People are missing. We don’t walk away from that.”

Kennedy didn’t argue, Oko just stared into the treeline. The moment they stepped into the Candin Wilderness, something felt wrong, the air was too still. No birds. No wind. Even their footsteps seemed… quieter.

“Forests aren’t supposed to be this quiet,” Twig muttered.

“Stay focused,” Lillian replied, though her grip tightened on her sword.

Oko paused, looking around slowly. “It’s watching.”

Twig blinked. “…what is?”

Oko didn’t answer. They found the caravan before nightfall, or what was left of it. Wagons overturned. Supplies scattered. There was blood everywhere.

Lillian knelt, her expression tightening. “…this was a fight.”

Twig looked around uneasily. “Yeah. And whatever did this… didn’t leave anything alive.”

Kennedy crouched near a deep gouge in the wood of a wagon. Claw marks.

The Entity stirred faintly. “Predators.”

Just then a long, echoing howl that rolled through the trees.

Twig went still. “…that’s not good.”

Another howl answered it, even closer to the party.

Lillian stood, raising her blade. “Form up.”

Oko’s voice was barely above a whisper. “…there’s more than one.”

It started as a blur between trees. Then, impact a massive shape slammed into Twig, sending him crashing to the ground.

“Contact!” he grunted, scrambling back.

The creature stepped into view—towering, fur matted with blood, eyes burning with hunger. A werewolf. Then another, and another. They were hunting the party. Lillian struck first, her blade cutting across one of the beasts—but the wound closed quickly after the cut. “They’re not going down!”

Twig slashed from the side, “Yeah, I noticed!”

Oko raised her hand, acid splashing across one— it hurt the werewolf a lot but also seemed to anger it further. “Magic works!”

Kennedy stands as a voice in his head rings out, “So much life…”

He struck. The werewolf staggered as something was pulled from it, but there were just so many of them. 

“Run!” Kennedy shouted

The Werewolves begin to change tactics, circling them and cutting off escape 

“They’re herding us!” Twig shouted, backing up.

“To where?” Lillian demanded.

They broke into a clearing—and froze. Bones piece of the torn caravan, and survivors.

A few of them—injured, barely alive left as play things for the Werewolves. They were headed right into the den of the werewolf leader.

“Help us…” one survivor croaked.

Lillian stepped forward immediately. “We’ve got you—”

The growl cut her off, as the alpha stepped out. A massive beast with heavy scars all across his body. The alpha let out a blood curtailing howl as the pack attacked all at once. 

Lillian held the front, barely keeping the alpha at bay. “Get them out of here!”

Twig tried to reach the survivors—but another werewolf jumped from the trees, claws tearing across his side.

Oko panicked as one lunged for her—“No—stay back!”—her magic flaring wildly but inconsistently.

Kennedy fought harder now, drawing more from the Entity—but every time he dropped one, another took its place. One of the survivors screamed as a werewolf dragged them away. Lillian turned—too late.

“…we can’t win this,” Kennedy said.

Lillian didn’t look at him. “We don’t leave people behind.”

Another scream cut through the clearing.

Twig staggered. “We die if we stay!”

Oko looked between them, terrified. “…please…”

Kennedy made the call, “We leave. Now.”

Lillian hesitated, for just a second. Then she turned and ran with the rest. The howls followed them all the way to the edge of the forest, they didn’t stop until the trees thinned.

“We left them,” Lillian said, her voice hollow.

Twig pressed a hand to his wound. “We would’ve joined them.”

Oko stared back into the forest. “They’re still out there…”

“Lillian, my dear we didn't stand a chance. But we will go back. And that's a promise.” Kennedy said out of breath. 

The Frightful Four defeated and discouraged head to the close city of Antilla and to a Inn where they find a flyer asking for assistance to help take out vampires and save people, so they took the flyer and headed to help.
